MONASTERY

The only monastery
It is also located in Kara-Balta. At first there was a women's monastery, which was transferred to the subvillage of the Holy Kazan Monastery in Balykchy. A male monastery is gradually being formed in Kara-Balta.
And in the village of Belovodskoe there is a monument of wooden architecture - the church of Archangel Michael. In 1864 a Cossack post was located here to fight against the Kokand Khanate. Cossacks built a small chapel. Later, on the bank of the Ak-Suu River they erected a church made of bricks (in the south-eastern part of the churchyard there are remains of the foundation). But it was destroyed by the strongest earthquake in 1875.
The villagers began to collect money for the construction of a new church. An important help was provided by military doctor Vasily Mikhailovich Frunze - father of military commander Mikhail Frunze.
The efforts of the people collected 40 thousand rubles - colossal money for those times. The timber for the temple was brought from Issyk-Kul. The road back and forth took more than two months. The construction was finished in 1882, and three years later Mikhail Frunze was baptized in it.
