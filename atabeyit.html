ATA-BEYIT MEMORIAL COMPLEX

In the village of Chon-Tash, Alameda district, 30 kilometers from Bishkek, there is the Ata-Beyit complex, which was built in 2000 to commemorate the victims of Stalin's repression of the 1930s.
The bodies of 137 Kyrgyz people of 19 nationalities are buried here. Such prominent figures of the Kyrgyz Soviet Republic as Yusup Abdrakhmanov, Bayaly Isakeev, Kasym Tynystanov and Torokul Aitmatov were convicted and shot without trial.
The area of the memorial complex is 2 hectares of land. Inside there is a museum, a cultural composition and a memorial monument. The museum has documents of victims of repression and documents about the political situation in 1920-30.
In addition, there is a monument to the victims of the Kyrgyz uprising against the Russian Empire. In 2016 the monument was built in honor of the 100th anniversary of the victims of the uprising. Kyrgyz were forced to flee through the mountains to China and many died of hunger and cold on the passes of Pamir and Tien Shan.
In addition, the great writer Chingiz Aitmatov was buried in Ata Beyit.
The road leading to Ata-Beyit is very good and very popular among cycling enthusiasts. Every year in summer time the route of all bicycle tours includes this road.
The memorial complex is located 30 km from Bishkek and you can get there by route #295 for 30 soms ($0.5) or by cab.
